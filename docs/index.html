Travel & Accommodations

Unless you're driving the AlCan, out-of-towners will fly in to the Ted Stevens Anchorage International Airport (ANC). We suggest you start looking for tickets as soon as possible, since July is a popular tourist month!

How do you get around once you're here? We highly recommend you rent a vehicle--something you can do right from the airport--or carpool with a friend. Cars are the #1 way to get around in Alaska.

Eagle River has a nice variety of Airbnb and VRBO listings, along with a few hotels and B&Bs. It’s also just 25 minutes away from Anchorage (the BIG city), where you'll find an abundance of hotels.

FAQ

What's the weather like? What should I wear to the wedding?

July in Alaska can be a little rainy*, but temperatures are often in the 60s. We recommend bringing a light layer or shawl to the wedding. And since it will be in a yard, some shoes that can handle grass & gravel would be a good idea!

Suggested attire: picnic semi-formal. We've heard some guests will be wearing sundresses and sport coats.

*Crossing fingers for sun, but we have a tent either way!

Where should I park?

Since the ceremony will be in the backyard, we ask that you park on Lacey Dr (Parking is not allowed on N Eagle River Loop Rd). There are stairs to access the front yard reception area, but if you need help getting around, we can shuttle you!

I'm confused—what kind of meal will we be having at your reception?

Since our wedding begins later in the evening (7 PM), we recommend you eat an early dinner or very late lunch beforehand. Of course, we will have plenty of appetizers, cake, and punch for you at the reception!
